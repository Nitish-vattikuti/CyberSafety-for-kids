Use Strong Passwords

Always create passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using names or birthdays.

Don't Talk to Strangers Online

Never chat or share personal info with people you don't know in real life.

Keep Your Devices Updated

Regular updates help protect your devices from harmful software and hackers.

Be Careful What You Click

Don't click on pop-ups or links in emails from unknown sources. They might contain viruses.

Log Out of Accounts

Always log out of your accounts on shared or public computers to stay safe.

Keep Your Info Private

Never share your address, phone number, or school name on public websites or with strangers online.

Report Suspicious Activity

If you see something strange or feel uncomfortable online, tell a trusted adult or teacher right away.

Be Careful with Downloads

Only download apps, games, or files from trusted sources to avoid viruses and malware.

Be Kind Online

Treat others with respect and kindness on the internet, just like you would in real life.

Balance Your Screen Time

Take breaks and spend time offline with friends, family, and hobbies for a healthy balance.

Stay safe and smart on the internet!
